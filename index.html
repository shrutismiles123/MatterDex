CH 01
The Birth of the MatterDex

In the beginning, the universe was nothing but a vast, silent void — a darkness so complete it consumed all light. Then, in a moment of unimaginable energy, the Great Synthesis occurred. From this primordial explosion, the first building blocks of existence scattered across the cosmos like brilliant sparks across an infinite sky.

The scientists of the future, the Archivists of Matter, gathered these sparks into a single sacred tome — the MatterDex. This living encyclopedia catalogued every known form of matter in the universe, assigning each a guardian spirit, a Chubu, whose powers reflected the properties of the matter they represented.

The MatterDex was not merely a book. It pulsed with energy, hummed with knowledge, and glowed with the light of a thousand worlds. To open it was to peer into the very fabric of reality itself.

CH 02
The First Chubus Awaken

Deep within the MatterDex, the first Chubus stirred to life. Ferron, the iron guardian, was the first to emerge — a towering figure of dark metallic armour, his form carved from pure elemental iron, immovable and eternal. Where he walked, the ground rang with the sound of hammers on anvils.

Next came Oxyra, crackling with electric life force, essential and invisible, the breath of the world. Beside her rose Hydronix, the smallest yet most explosive of all the Chubus, dancing with blue fire that could power stars.

One by one, the guardians awakened. Each bore the mark of their matter — their strengths, their weaknesses, their alliances. Together, they formed the Council of Compounds, pledging to maintain the balance of matter across all dimensions.

CH 03
The Rust Phantom Rises

Not all compounds were born of noble purpose. When Ferron encountered the relentless Oxyra in a realm of water and air, something unexpected happened. Their energies clashed and merged, and from the battle emerged Rust Phantom — a creature of slow, creeping orange decay, born from the process of oxidation.

Rust Phantom was not evil, but he was relentless. Wherever he passed, iron weakened, bridges crumbled, and machines groaned. He moved silently, invisibly, working across years and centuries. Ferron feared no sword, no hammer — but Rust Phantom wore him down with nothing more than time, moisture, and air.

The appearance of Rust Phantom taught the Archivists a vital truth: matter changes. Nothing remains as it began. Even the mightiest guardian carries the seed of transformation within them.

CH 04
Aurex and the Electroplate Wars

Aurex, guardian of gold, was ancient beyond measure. Where others corroded and changed, Aurex remained radiant — noble, unreactive, eternal. The civilizations of the MatterDex world coveted Aurex's power, not just for wealth, but for protection.

The Archivists discovered that by channelling electric current through a solution of gold ions — a technique they called electroplating — they could coat weaker metals with Aurex's golden armour. Ferron, once vulnerable to Rust Phantom, could be shielded beneath a gleaming shell of gold.

The Electroplate Wars were fought not with weapons but with chemistry vats and electric coils, as the guardians competed to armour their chosen metals. In the end, Aurex stood above them all, untarnished and magnificent, a testament to the power of stability in a universe that never stops changing.

CH 05
The Mixtures and the Age of Chaos

Not all matter combined in perfect chemical bonds. Some existed in loose, chaotic arrangements — the Mixtures. Hemovex, guardian of blood, was the most complex of all: a dynamic mixture of plasma, cells, and proteins, constantly shifting, carrying life itself through every living system.

Where compounds were rigid and predictable, mixtures were fluid, versatile, and wild. They could be separated, filtered, and redistributed. They teamed together without changing their individual natures, a society of matter rather than a merger.

The Age of Chaos tested every guardian. The Chubus of mixtures rallied under Aqua, the water guardian, whose neutral nature made her the great mediator — dissolving conflicts, carrying solutions, sustaining all life that the MatterDex world had ever known.

CH 06
Uranion and the Final Frontier

At the very edge of the known world, beyond the safe zones of stable elements, lay the domain of Uranion — the heaviest, most powerful, and most dangerous of all the Chubus. Radioactive and unstable, Uranion contained the energy of stars compressed into matter, slowly releasing it across millennia.

The Archivists approached Uranion with awe and caution. His power was immense — capable of lighting entire cities, or of catastrophic destruction if mishandled. He was the final lesson of the MatterDex: that matter is neither good nor bad, but that understanding it makes all the difference.

With the full Council assembled, the MatterDex was complete. The Guardians of Matter stood ready — a living bridge between science and story, between knowledge and wonder. And so the adventure begins for every student brave enough to open the first page.

⚛️
ELEMENTS
An element is a pure substance made of only one kind of atom. Elements cannot be broken down into simpler substances by chemical means. There are 118 known elements, each listed on the Periodic Table. Each element has a unique atomic number representing the number of protons in its nucleus.
◈ MatterDex Link: Ferron = Iron (Fe), Aurex = Gold (Au), Hydronix = Hydrogen (H), Oxyra = Oxygen (O), Uranion = Uranium (U)
🧪
COMPOUNDS
A compound is a substance formed when two or more elements are chemically bonded together. Compounds have fixed compositions and properties different from their component elements. They can only be separated by chemical reactions. Water (H₂O) and rust (Fe₂O₃) are classic examples.
◈ MatterDex Link: Rust Phantom = Iron Oxide (Fe₂O₃), Aqua = Water (H₂O), Hemovex = Blood (complex biological compound-mixture)
🌀
MIXTURES
A mixture is a combination of two or more substances not chemically combined. Each component retains its own properties and can be separated by physical methods like filtration, evaporation, or distillation. Mixtures can be homogeneous (uniform) or heterogeneous (non-uniform).
◈ Types include: solutions, suspensions, colloids. Blood is a complex heterogeneous mixture containing plasma, red/white blood cells, and platelets.
🟠
RUSTING
Rusting is the process by which iron reacts with oxygen and water to form iron oxide (rust). It is an example of a chemical change and oxidation reaction. The balanced chemical equation is:

4Fe + 3O₂ + 6H₂O → 4Fe(OH)₃ → 2Fe₂O₃·3H₂O
◈ Prevention: painting, galvanising, oiling, and electroplating with corrosion-resistant metals like gold or zinc.
⚡
ELECTROPLATING
Electroplating is the process of depositing a thin layer of one metal onto another using electrolysis. An electric current passes through a solution containing ions of the coating metal. The object to be coated is the cathode; the coating metal is the anode.
◈ Applications: gold-plated jewellery, chrome car bumpers, silver cutlery, and zinc-coated (galvanised) steel to prevent rusting.
